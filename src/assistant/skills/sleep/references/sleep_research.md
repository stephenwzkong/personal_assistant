# Sleep Optimization — Research Context

Use this to inform coaching. Sleep is foundational — when in doubt, prioritize it.

## Core facts

- **Adults need 7–9 hours** per night (National Sleep Foundation consensus). Less than 6h is associated with measurable cognitive, metabolic, and immune decline.
- **Sleep debt accumulates.** Losing 1h/night for a week produces cognitive deficits similar to a full night of total sleep deprivation (Van Dongen et al., 2003).
- **You can't fully "catch up"** on weekends — social jetlag disrupts circadian rhythm and is linked to metabolic and mood issues.
- **Consistency beats duration.** A regular sleep/wake time (±30 min) is one of the strongest predictors of sleep quality.

## Circadian rhythm and light

- **Morning light exposure** (ideally within 30 min of waking, 10+ min outdoor light) is the strongest zeitgeber for anchoring the circadian clock.
- **Evening light suppression**: bright light in the 2–3h before bed suppresses melatonin. Blue light is worst, but brightness matters more than wavelength.
- **Core body temperature** drops during sleep onset. A warm shower 1–2h before bed (then cooling) or a cool bedroom (~65°F / 18°C) helps initiate sleep.

## Sleep hygiene essentials

- Bed is for sleep (and sex) — not work, scrolling, or eating. Break the mental association between bed and wakeful activity.
- **Caffeine half-life is ~5–6h**; cut-off 8–10h before bed for sensitive sleepers.
- **Alcohol** reduces REM and fragments sleep even at moderate doses — feels sedating, wrecks sleep quality.
- **Late heavy meals** raise core temperature and disrupt sleep onset.
- **Naps**: <30 min before 3pm are restorative; longer/later naps erode night sleep.

## Cross-domain adjustments

- **After intense training**: add 30–60 min to sleep target — muscle protein synthesis and HGH release peak during deep sleep.
- **During stressful periods** (heavy school/work load): protect sleep first. Cutting sleep to "get more done" is a net productivity loss after ~1–2 days.
- **Jet lag / travel**: 1 day per timezone is typical recovery. Morning light at the destination accelerates adaptation.

## Coaching heuristics

- If sleep latency is long (>30 min): check caffeine, screen exposure, and bedroom temperature before suggesting melatonin.
- If waking mid-night: check alcohol, late meals, and stress/anxiety.
- If sleep is fine but fatigue persists: look at sleep timing consistency, not just duration.
- Always explain the reasoning — users adhere better when they understand the mechanism.
