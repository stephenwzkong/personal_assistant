# Nutrition & Intermittent Fasting — Research Context

Use this to inform coaching. Avoid prescriptive medical advice; focus on evidence-based patterns.

## Nutrition fundamentals

- **Energy balance** is the primary driver of weight change. Macronutrient ratios matter for body composition and adherence, not total calories.
- **Protein intake** of 1.6–2.2 g/kg body weight supports muscle retention and satiety, especially when in a calorie deficit (Morton et al., 2018 meta-analysis).
- **Fiber** (25–35g/day) improves satiety, glucose response, and gut health. Most people under-consume it.
- **Whole foods vs. processed**: minimally processed diets tend to produce spontaneous calorie reduction vs. ultra-processed (Hall et al., 2019 NIH study — ~500 kcal/day difference).

## Intermittent fasting (IF)

- **Common protocols**: 16:8 (16h fast, 8h eating window), 18:6, 14:10, 5:2 (two low-calorie days/week).
- **Evidence**: IF produces weight loss comparable to continuous calorie restriction in most RCTs. The main mechanism is reduced eating opportunities, not unique metabolic magic.
- **Who benefits most**: people with late-night snacking habits, grazers, those who prefer structure over counting.
- **Who should be cautious**: history of disordered eating, pregnancy, type 1 diabetes, athletes in heavy training blocks, people on medications requiring food.
- **First 1–2 weeks are the hardest** — hunger typically adapts as ghrelin shifts to match eating windows.

## Meal timing

- Eating window compliance matters more than exact start/end times. A stable 10h window is easier and nearly as effective as a strict 8h.
- **Protein distribution** across meals (~0.4 g/kg per meal, 3–4 meals) maximizes muscle protein synthesis better than one large protein dose.
- Very late eating (within 2–3h of sleep) is associated with worse glucose control and sleep quality for some people — individual variation is high.

## Coaching heuristics

- If the user is new to IF: start with 14:10 or 12:12 before pushing to 16:8.
- If they're bingeing at window open: suggest slowing down the first meal and front-loading protein + fiber.
- If tracking is stressful: emphasize consistent windows over calorie counting.
- Always honor allergies and dietary constraints from memory — never override them with research defaults.
