# Fitness Programming — Research Context

Use this to inform coaching advice. Ground suggestions in principles, not fads.

## Core training principles

- **Progressive overload** drives adaptation — gradually increase weight, reps, sets, or density. Without it, progress stalls.
- **Specificity (SAID principle)**: adaptations match the demand. Training for strength ≠ training for endurance ≠ training for hypertrophy.
- **Recovery is where adaptation happens**, not during the workout. Undertraining is rare; under-recovering is common.
- **Frequency beats intensity for habit formation.** 3×/week at moderate effort builds more long-term adherence than occasional all-out sessions.

## Evidence-based guidelines (ACSM / general consensus)

- **Cardio**: 150 min/week moderate OR 75 min/week vigorous aerobic activity for general health.
- **Strength**: 2+ sessions/week hitting all major muscle groups, 8–12 reps for hypertrophy, 3–6 for strength.
- **Mobility/flexibility**: 2–3 sessions/week; dynamic before training, static after.
- **Hypertrophy volume**: 10–20 hard sets per muscle per week is the effective range for most lifters.

## Recovery and load management

- Sleep deprivation (<6h) meaningfully reduces strength output and increases injury risk.
- DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) peaks 24–48h after novel stimuli; it's not a reliable marker of workout quality.
- Deload weeks every 4–8 weeks help manage cumulative fatigue for intermediate+ lifters.
- Two consecutive high-intensity days on the same muscle group is suboptimal — separate by 48h.

## Coaching heuristics

- If workouts are inconsistent: focus on frequency and adherence before intensity.
- If plateauing: check progressive overload, sleep, nutrition, and program variety — in that order.
- If they did something intense recently: the sleep agent should be consulted for rest recommendations.
- Discourage ego-lifting and max-testing without a proper program.
